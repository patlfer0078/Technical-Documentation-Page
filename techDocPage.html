Introduction

The Texas A&M-Chevron Engineering Academy at Texas Southmost College is an innovative co-enrollment program that provides a pathway to students interested in pursuing an engineering degree at Texas A&M University. Students admitted to the Engineering Academy at Texas Southmost College are Texas A&M engineering students co-enrolled at Texas A&M University in the College of Engineering and at Texas Southmost College.

Benefits of the Texas A&M Engineering Academy at Texas Southmost College include:

  • Being co-enrolled at Texas A&M University and Texas Southmost College. Engineering students in the Engineering Academy program enroll in one engineering course through Texas A&M each semester. These courses are taught by Texas A&M faculty on the Texas Southmost College campus. All math, science and other core curriculum courses are taken through Texas Southmost College;
  • Smaller student-faculty ratios in math and science courses, which build the foundation in engineering;
  • Automatic admission to the first choice engineering major at the end of the second semester for engineering students who earn a minimum 3.5 cumulative grade point average (CGPA) at both Texas Southmost College and Texas A&M and who complete the required coursework

    Applying

    The process for applying to the Engineering Academy takes 2 steps

    1. Apply to Texas Southmost College via ApplyTexas and click "Yes" when asked if you are interested in the Engineering Academy
      • Your application will be sent to the Engineering Academy office and screened for prequalification
      • Once determined to qualify, you will be sent a unique link containing the application for the Texas A&M Engineering Academies site
    2. Complete your Texas A&M Engineering Academy application and submit the proper documentation

    What additional documentation can I expect Texas A&M to require?

    First-Time-in-College Students (requirements for students without college credit or those whose only college credit was earned prior to high school graduation — i.e. dual credit, AP/IB or early college high school)

    • Official High School Transcript
    • Texas A&M Residency Questionnaire
    • SAT/ACT Scores (These must be sent by the testing agency and not the high school.)
    • Bacterial Meningitis Proof of Vaccination (if admitted)
    • Final High School Transcript (if admitted)

    Continuing College Students (requirements for students who have earned college credits after earning a high school diploma):

    • Official College Transcripts (Every institution the student attends — including institutions where dual credit was earned — must submit transcripts).
    • Texas A&M Residency Questionnaire
    • Bacterial Meningitis Proof of Vaccination (if admitted)
    • Final High School Transcript (if admitted)

    How to Qualify

    Eligibility

    The Texas A&M-Chevron Engineering Academy at Texas Southmost College is for first-time, full-time students and continuing students who have achieved a high school diploma or equivalent and are pre-calculus or calculus ready as determined by Texas Southmost College.

    • Standardized Test Scores
      1. Minimum 22 in Math on ACT, or
      2. Minimum 550 in Math on SAT
    • Credit for MATH 1314 or higher on current TSC transcript OR
    • Transfer credit for MATH 1314 or higher from another institution OR
    • Credit by exam (AP, CLEP, IB) for MATH 1314 or higher OR
    • Valid score on internal math assessment test for MATH 1314 (Students need a 70 or higher)

    Please note that students must be ready to enroll in MATH 2412 (Precalculus) or higher beginning in Fall 2020.

    TSC will start prequalifying students by December. The Texas A&M Engineering Academy application opens January 1, 2020. The Engineering Academy application closes June 30, 2020. The deadline to submit supporting credentials (test scores, transcripts, etc.) is July 13, 2020. Detailed information about this process can be found on the Texas A&M Engineering Academy application student guide.

    The Office of Admissions at Texas A&M will begin making admission decisions in April 2020. Successful applicants will receive an offer of admission from Texas A&M University to the Engineering Academy at Texas Southmost College and will be advised on how to accept the offer and register for courses. Students will have until August 1, 2020 to accept the offer.

    Students admitted and enrolled in the Texas A&M-Chevron Engineering Academy program are engineering students in the College of Engineering at Texas A&M University and enrolled at Texas Southmost College. Once enrolled, all courses must be completed through Texas Southmost College and Texas A&M University-College Station.
